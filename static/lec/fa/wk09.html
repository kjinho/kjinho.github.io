Generative Artificial Intelligence

A. What is Generative AI?

  • ChatGPT:

    • “Generative AI is a type of artificial intelligence that can create new content—such as text, images, music, video, or code—by learning patterns from existing data. Instead of just analyzing or classifying information, generative AI systems generate original outputs that resemble human-created work.”

  • Gemini:

    • “Generative Artificial Intelligence (Generative AI or GenAI) is a subfield of AI that focuses on creating new content and data in response to a user's input or prompt.”

  • Copilot:

    • “Generative AI is a type of artificial intelligence that creates new content—like text, images, music, or code—based on patterns it has learned from existing data.”

B. How does GenAI work?

  • Neural networks

  • Training data and back propagation

  • Responses based on prompts and training data

C. What do lawyers use GenAI for?

  • Drafting

  • Research

  • Reviewing contracts

  • Data analysis

  • Brainstorming

  • Reviewing discovery

  • Summarizing documents

  • Assisting with communication

D. Concerns with GenAI

  • Bias

  • Mindless parroting of training input

  • Hallucinations

  • Black box

  • Not necessarily aligned with human values

  • Plagiarism, copyright, and intellectual property infringement concerns

  • Cost to train

  • Cost to operate

  • Failure to update training data with new knowledge

  • Confidentiality

E. Noteworthy GenAI Failures

  • Mata v. Avianca, Inc., 678 F. Supp. 3d 443, 448–49 (S.D.N.Y. 2023) (sanctioning attorneys under Fed. R. Civ. P. 11 for “abandon[ing] their responsibilities when they submitted non-existent judicial opinions with fake quotes and citations created by the artificial intelligence tool ChatGPT, then continued to stand by the fake opinions after judicial orders called their existence into question”).

  • Ex parte Lee, 673 S.W.3d 755, 756 (Tex. App. 2023) (“Lee cites to five cases to support the two sub-arguments to his issue. Only three of those five cases are published. None of the three published cases cited actually exist in the Southwest Reporter.”)

  • Shahid v. Esaam, 376 Ga. App. 145, 145, 918 S.E.2d 198, 199 (2025) (“The trial court denied the motion, using an order that relied upon non-existent case law. For the reasons discussed below, we vacate the order and remand for the trial court to hold a new hearing on Wife’s petition.”)
