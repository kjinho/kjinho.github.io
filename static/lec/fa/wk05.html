Example for Discussion: Citations

In Cheesman v. Tennessee Student Assistance Corp., the Sixth Circuit found that the Cheesmans could not maintain a minimal standard of living for their family if they were required to repay their loans. See Cheesman v. Tenn. Student Assistance Corp. (In re Cheesman), 25 F.3d 356, 359 (6th Cir. 1994). The Cheesmans’ 1992 gross income exceeded by only a slim margin the government’s 1992 poverty income guideline. Id. And based on their current situation and employment history, there was no assurance that their financial condition would improve in the foreseeable future. Id. at 360. This was one of the main reasons why the court held that the Cheesmans student loans were dischargeable. Id. at 359–60.
