IRAC

  • What? IRAC is a basic building block for all legal writing.

  • How? Issue, Rule, Application, Conclusion1

Topic Sentences

  • Every paragraph must begin with a topic sentence.

  • Every sentence in the paragraph must relate directly to the idea expressed in the topic sentence.

Issue Statements

  • An issue statement can serve as a topic sentence.

  • Two ways to formulate issue statements:

    1. Stating an unanswered question

    2. Stating a thesis

Under/Does/When Format

“Under [legal standard], does/has [legal question] when [legally significant facts]?”

  • legal standard: applicable law

  • legal question: the question that needs answering

  • legally significant facts: facts material to the question under the standard

Example:

  • Background: “Your client was traveling to LA and was accused of taking his neighbor’s car without his knowledge or consent. He was charged with violating the California Penal Code.”

  • Issue Statement: “Under the California Penal Code, did the defendant commit theft when he took his neighbor’s car without notice or permission?”

Whether Format

The issue is whether [legal question] under [controlling law].

Examples:

  • The issue is whether the defendant committed theft when he took his neighbor’s car without notice or permission.

  • The issue is whether the defendant committed theft under the California Penal Code.

  • The issue is whether the defendant committed theft under the California Penal Code when he took his neighbor’s car without notice or permission.

Thesis Format

Under [legal standard], [legal answer] when [legally significant facts].

Example: Under the California Penal Code, the defendant committed theft when he took his neighbor’s car without notice or permission.

Rule Synthesis

  • Rule (IRAC)

    • State the Rule

    • Explain the Rule (case law application of the rule)

  • Rule synthesis is the process of combining multiple sources of law to create a single, coherent rule that can be applied to a particular legal issue.

Office Memorandum

  • Who is the audience?

  • What is its purpose?

  • What form does it take?

Bouchoux discusses the office memorandum at length.2

Parts of the Office Memorandum

Question Presented

  • Identifies the relevant legal theory

  • Includes case-specific facts that give the question helpful context

  • Is answerable “yes” or “no”

Brief Answer

  • Responds to the question presented

  • Concisely uses specific facts to explain the reason for this conclusion

  • Organized to mirror the elements of the applicable legal theory

Statement of Facts

  • Contains everything a reader needs to understand the factual circumstance of the matter

  • Includes a brief introductory paragraph that identifies the parties and the nature of the dispute

  • Organizes the facts clearly—typically in chronological order

  • Includes all facts that the discussion relies

  • Omits irrelevant and unnecessary information

Discussion

  • Organized using IRAC

  • Begins with an introductory paragraph that states the issue and the applicable rule

  • “Roadmaps” the analysis

  • Begins each paragraph with a topic sentence

  • Includes a separate IRAC for each legal element that requires analysis

  • Analyzes the problem thoroughly

  • Cites to authority, including pinpoint citations, wherever necessary

Conclusion

  • Answers all questions presented clearly and succinctly

  • Contains relevant and helpful questions or recommendations for the reader
