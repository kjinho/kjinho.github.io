How a Case Becomes Case Law

  1. A dispute arises.

  2. Plaintiff sues Defendant in a trial court by filing a complaint.

  3. The trial court decides the case and enters judgment.

  4. The losing party appeals.

  5. An appellate court decides the case AND publishes an opinion.

IRAC

  • What? IRAC is a basic building block for all legal writing.

  • How? Issue, Rule, Application, Conclusion

Bouchoux contains a brief discussion of IRAC.5

Issue

Formed as either

  • an identification of the issue (“The issue is whether Mr. Doe’s statement is admissible as an excited utterance ....”) or

  • a thesis statement (“Mr. Doe’s statement is admissible as an excited utterance because ....”).

Rule

Contains two parts:

  1. State the Rule

  2. Explain the Rule (case law application of the rule)

    • What was the case outcome?

    • What material facts led to that outcome?

Application

  1. Apply the rule to the facts

  2. Compare the facts with case law facts (e.g., “similarly,” “by contrast”)

Conclusion/Thesis

State or restate your conclusion in one sentence.

Assignment Formatting

  • All written work must be typed.

  • Paper Size: Each page must be eight and one-half inches in width and eleven inches in height (NOT A4).

  • Margins:

    • The top and bottom margins of each page must be at least one inch.

    • The left and right margins of each page must be at least one inch.

    • All text, except page numbers, must appear within these margins.

  • Typeface: The typeface must be a serifed font of 12 point size or larger.

    • Examples: Book Antiqua, New Century Schoolbook, Times New Roman

    • NOT: Arial, Helvetica

  • Spacing:

    • Text must be double-spaced, except that argument headings, footnotes and indented quotations may be single-spaced.

    • For purposes of this rule, single spacing means not more than six lines of text per vertical inch; double spacing means not more than three lines of text per vertical inch and not more than twenty-seven double-spaced lines on a page.
