1.2. General Considerations

  • Capture the intent of the parties
  • Avoid making business decisions
  • Attempt to prevent future problems

1.3. Drafting Considerations

  • Start with a form
  • Comply with the applicable law
  • Be specific
  • Use headings, tables, and attachments

2. Ethical Considerations

2.1. Conflicts of Interest

When preparing transactional documents, always keep in mind who the client is. If several parties (e.g., co-founders of a business) ask you to draft a document for them, it may be necessary to advise them of the potential conflict of interest and to obtain a written waiver of the conflict.

ABA Model R. Prof. C. 1.7, 1.8

3.1. Grading Criteria

  • Overall Appearance
    • [ ] Does the document include all necessary sections?
    • [ ] Is the typing and physical presentation neat and in the necessary format?
    • [ ] Did the student comply with the mandatory time limit?
  • Discussion
    • [ ] Does the discussion begin with appropriate introductory paragraphs?
    • [ ] Does the discussion end with appropriate concluding paragraphs?
    • [ ] Does each paragraph have a topic sentence?
    • [ ] Are the paragraphs too long (more than 2/3 or 3/4 page)?
    • [ ] Does the writer analyze the problem thoroughly?
    • [ ] Does the writer support every legal statement with a citation to legal authority?
    • [ ] Does the writer use the TREAT method consistently, setting forth rules, explaining the cases, applying the rules, and coming to a conclusion for each issue?
  • Notice of Meeting
    • [ ] Does the notice of meeting comply with the relevant law?
    • [ ] Does the notice of meeting reflect the clients’ requirements?
  • Style
    • [ ] Did the writer review the paper for spelling, grammar, and typing errors?
    • [ ] Do the citations comply with Bluebook form?

3.2.1. Memorandum

To: Law Clerk
From: Bond Forger, Partner
Subject: Vectar Inc.
Date: April 6, 2022

One of the shareholders of Vectar Inc. recently approached us with questions about the effectiveness of a recent vote to remove Jason Smalls as the president of the company.

Vectar Inc. is a Massachusetts domestic corporation. It has five shareholders, each of whom owns exactly twenty percent of the outstanding shares. The shares entitle each of the shareholders to vote at meetings of shareholders. The names of the shareholders are as follows:

  • Jason Smalls
  • Julia Cannon
  • Stanley Roy
  • Samson Coffee
  • Sarah Knapp

The company was founded three years ago. It is an online software publisher. The company has fifteen employees and has annual revenues of roughly three million dollars. The company’s principal office is 123 Main Street, Plainview, Massachusetts.

Since its founding, the directors of the corporation have been Jason, Julia, and Stanley. The officers of the corporation have been Jason as the president, Julia as the treasurer, and Stanley as the secretary and clerk.

The company has articles of organization but no bylaws.

Recently, Stanley, Samson, and Sarah have been unhappy with Jason’s conduct as president of the company. Specifically, they have received complaints from several employees that Jason has been sexually harassing them. Despite having had several conversations with Jason about the problem, Stanley, Samson, and Sarah have been unsuccessful in changing his behavior.

Not wanting to wait until the next shareholder or director meeting to fire Jason, Stanley and Samson and Sarah prepared a signed a document entitled “Written Shareholder Consent.” A copy of this document is enclosed. They signed the document without notice to the other shareholders, and without a meeting of shareholders.

We have serious doubts about the legal effectiveness of the Written Shareholder Consent. Please draft the discussion section of an office memo explaining why the Written Shareholder Consent is ineffective to remove Jason as president of Vectar Inc. Please also draft a notice of special meeting to remove Jason as president of Vectar Inc.

3.2.8. Mass. Gen. Laws ch. 156D, § 8.08

Section 8.21. ACTION WITHOUT MEETING

(a) … action required or permitted by this chapter to be taken by the directors … may be taken without a meeting if the action is taken by the unanimous consent of the members of the board of directors. The action must be evidenced by 1 or more consents describing the action taken, in writing, signed by each director, or delivered to the corporation by electronic transmission, to the address specified by the corporation for the purpose or, if no address has been specified, to the principal office of the corporation, addressed to the secretary or other officer or agent having custody of the records of proceedings of directors, and included in the minutes or filed with the corporate records reflecting the action taken.

(b) Action taken under this section is effective when the last director signs or delivers the consent, unless the consent specifies a different effective date.

(c) A consent signed or delivered under this section has the effect of a meeting vote and may be described as such in any document.

3.2.11. Vectar Inc. Articles of Organization

  1. Article I

    The exact name of the corporation is Vectar Inc.

  2. Article II

    The corporation has the purpose of engaging in any lawful business.

  3. Article III

    The corporation is authorized to issue a total of 50,000 shares of no Par common stock. A total of 5,000 of these shares are outstanding.

  4. Article IV

    There is only one class of shares.

  5. Article V

    There are no restrictions on transfer of shares.

  6. Article VI

    The size of the board of directors shall be fixed at three. If at any time there is a vacancy on the board of directors, the vacancy shall be filled by vote of a majority of the directors then in office.

  7. Article VII

    The annual meeting of shareholders shall be held at the third Monday of January at the corporation’s principal office.

  8. Signed on January 22, 2019 by the incorporators.
