Contracts

  • Exchange of promises

Negotiations

  • Preparation
    • Understand your client’s concerns and goals
    • Research as necessary
  • Negotiating
    • Find ways to agree
    • Focus on interests, not positions
    • Avoid making business decisions for the client
    • Avoid overreaching
    • Lopsided agreements lead to default

Drafting Transactional Documents

  • Drafters frequently rely on forms and models
    • e.g., American Jurisprudence Legal Forms, Second Edition
  • Documents tend to be long and detailed
  • Good transactional documents are unambiguous, well organized, and accurately reflect the parties’ agreement

Reviewing and Proofreading Transactional Documents

  • Does the document reflect the client’s goals and expectations?
  • Are any provisions ambiguous?
  • Are there any missing provisions?

Parts of a Typical Transactional Document

Introductory Matter

  • Name of document
  • Parties
  • Effective date

SETTLEMENT AGREEMENT AND RELEASE

On January 13, 2013, Wile E. Coyote (“Plaintiff” or “Coyote”) and ACME Corporation (“Defendant” or “ACME”) (collectively the “Parties”) hereby make and enter into this Settlement Agreement and Release (this “Settlement Agreement”).

Recitals

  • Indicate the purpose of the document

WHEREAS, a dispute has arisen between Coyote and ACME concerning injuries Coyote sustained from dynamite he purchased from ACME; ...

Consideration

  • Contracts frequently include a statement about consideration below the recitals.

NOW THEREFORE, in consideration for the mutual promises contained herein, and for the good and valuable consideration recited herein, the receipt and sufficiency of which is hereby acknowledged, the Parties agree as follows ....

Operative Terms

  • Use descriptive headings for each section
  • If necessary, include a separate alphabetized section of definitions at the beginning.
  • If necessary, move long lists of items out of the body of the agreement and into attachments to the agreement.
  • The terms should accurately reflect the understanding of the parties.

Standard Provisions (1/3)

  • Applicable law
    • This agreement and its enforcement, and any controversy arising out of or relating to the making or performance of this agreement, shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the law of the state of New York, without regard to New York’s principles of conflicts of law.

Standard Provisions (2/3)

  • Venue
    • The parties agree to submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the Commercial Division of New York State Supreme Court or the federal courts in New York State, which shall hear any dispute, claim, or controversy arising in connection with or relating to this agreement, including but not limited to the validity, breach, enforcment, or termination thereof.

Standard Provisions (3/3)

  • Severability clause
  • Notice provisions
  • Other boilerplate

Signatures

  • Make sure the right people are signing, particularly on behalf of a corporation
  • Sometimes lawyers will sign an agreement to indicate that they have “Approved [the agreement] as to form”

Negotiation and Drafting Assignment Grading Rubric

Substantive Provisions

  1. The authors include all of the relevant, material facts in the recitations
  2. The authors adequately define any terms in the agreement
  3. The agreement clearly sets forth the obligations of the parties
  4. The agreement accurately reflects the intent of the parties
  5. The agreement covers all the points agreed upon by the parties in their prior negotiations
  6. The agreement considers any possible contingencies
  7. The agreement is reasonably fair to both parties, such that the parties are likely to comply with the agreement
  8. The agreement contains the necessary boilerplate

Style

  1. The agreement uses clear and consistent language
  2. The agreement contains no ambiguities
  3. The authors use an appropriate format for the agreement
  4. The agreement contains no typographical or spelling errors
