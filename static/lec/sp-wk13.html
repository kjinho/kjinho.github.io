1. Writing Resources

1.1. Books on Writing

  • Ross Guberman, Point Made (2011).
  • Stephen King, On Writing (2000).
  • Bryan A. Garner, The Winning Brief (3d ed. 2014).

1.2. Style and Grammar Guides

  • The Chicago Manual of Style (17th ed.).
  • William Strunk Jr. & E.B. White, The Elements of Style (4th ed. 2000).
  • Patricia T. O’Conner, Woe Is I (1996).

1.3. Read (anything)!

  • English-language news sources (e.g., New York Times, Wall Street Journal, ProPublica, USA Today)
  • Biographies, memoirs, and other non-fiction (e.g., Ibram X. Kendi’s How to Be an Antiracist, Ta-Nehisi Coates’s Between the World and Me, Ron Chernow’s Alexander Hamilton, Michelle Obama’s Becoming, Michelle Kuo’s Reading with Patrick)
  • Fiction

2. Research and Investigation

2.1. Learn the Law

  • Secondary sources
    • colleagues
    • treatises
    • articles
    • commercial outlines
  • Primary source research
    • constitutions, statutes, regulations, rules
    • cases

2.2. Ethics for Legal Research

  • ABA Model R. Prof. C.
    Rule 1.1
    A lawyer shall provide competent representation to a client. Competent representation requires the legal knowledge, skill, thoroughness and preparation reasonably necessary for the representation.
    Rule 1.3
    A lawyer shall act with reasonable diligence and promptness in representing a client.
    Rule 2.1
    In representing a client, a lawyer shall exercise independent professional judgment and render candid advice. In rendering advice, a lawyer may refer not only to law but to other considerations such as moral, economic, social and political factors, that may be relevant to the client’s situation.

2.3. Investigate the Facts

  • Review provided facts
  • Understand the scope of the facts
  • “It’s not about what I believe; it’s about what I can prove.”

2.4. Ethics for Facts and Investigations

  • ABA Model R. Prof. C.
    Rule 3.1
    A lawyer shall not bring or defend a proceeding, or assert or controvert an issue therein, unless there is a basis in law and fact for doing so that is not frivolous ….
    Rule 3.3(a)
    A lawyer shall not knowingly … make a false statement of fact or law to a tribunal or fail to correct a false statement of material fact or law previously made to the tribunal by the lawyer; … [or] offer evidence that the lawyer knows to be false….
