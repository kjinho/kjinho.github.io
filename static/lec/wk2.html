Writing: Fundamental Goals

Avoiding Passive Voice

Passive Voice: Example 1

  • Passive: The ball was thrown by Adam. (6)
  • Active: Adam threw the ball. (4)

Passive Voice: Example 2

  • Passive: Presented with this evidence, the court was satisfied that the plaintiff’s burden of proof had been met. (17)
  • Active: This evidence satisfied the court that the plaintiff had met her burden of proof. (14)
  • Active: The court found that the evidence satisfied the plaintiff’s burden of proof. (12)
  • Active: The plaintiff presented enough evidence to satisfy her burden of proof. (11)
  • Active: The plaintiff satisfied her burden of proof. (7)

IRAC (25 minutes)

  • What? IRAC is a basic building block for all legal writing.
  • How? Issue, Rule, Application, Conclusion

Bouchoux contains a brief discussion of IRAC.

Issue

Formed as either

  • an identification of the issue (“The issue is whether Mr. Doe’s statement is admissible as an excited utterance ....”) or
  • a thesis statement (“Mr. Doe’s statement is admissible as an excited utterance because ....”).

Rule

  1. State the Rule
  2. Explain the Rule (case law application of the rule)
    • What was the case outcome?
    • What material facts led to that outcome?

Application

  1. Apply the rule to the facts
  2. Compare the facts with case law facts (e.g., “similarly,” “by contrast”)

Conclusion/Thesis

State or restate your conclusion.

IRAC Exercise

On September 15, 2020, Ben, a 28-year-old law student, was stopped by BU Police while riding his rented blue bicycle on the sidewalk on Commonwealth Avenue. Ben was charged with a violation of the Road Traffic Act, 1985, Section 18.

SHOULD HE PAY THE $250 FINE OR CONTEST THE CHARGES?

The Road Traffic Act, 1985, Sec. 18

  • (a) It is a violation of this law for any person to drive any vehicle on any footpath;
  • (b) For purposes of this law, the following words are defined as follows:
    1. a “person” is any human being over the age of 16 years old;
    2. a “vehicle” is any method of mechanical transportation;

    3. to “drive” a vehicle means to control how fast the vehicle goes and the direction the vehicle goes in; and

    4. a “footpath” is any sidewalk or path that is intended primarily for people to walk on, not for vehicles;
  • (c) The penalty for any violation of this law shall be a fine of $250 USD.
