IRAC (25 minutes)

  • What? IRAC is a basic building block for all legal writing.
  • How? Issue, Rule, Application, Conclusion

Bouchoux contains a brief discussion of IRAC.

Issue

Formed as either

  • an identification of the issue (“The issue is whether Mr. Doe’s statement is admissible as an excited utterance ....”) or
  • a thesis statement (“Mr. Doe’s statement is admissible as an excited utterance because ....”).

Rule

  1. State the Rule
  2. Explain the Rule (case law application of the rule)
    • What was the outcome in the case law?
    • What material facts led to that outcome?

Application

  1. Apply the rule to the facts of the present case
  2. Compare the facts with case law facts (e.g., “similarly,” “by contrast”)

Conclusion/Thesis

State or restate your conclusion.

Sample outline (2-prongs)

  • Paragraph 1 (IRA-)
    • overarching Issue
    • state the Rule (with both factors)
    • summarize Application of rule and transition to detailed discussion to follow
  • Paragraph 2 (IRAC for factor 1)
    • Issue for factor 1
    • state the Rule for factor 1
    • Apply factor 1
    • Conclude factor 1
  • Paragraph 3 (IRAC for factor 2)
    • Issue for factor 2
    • state the Rule for factor 2
    • Apply factor 2
    • Conclude factor 2
  • Paragraph 4 (-C): Conclude from application of factors 1 and 2

Office Memorandum (10 minutes)

  • Who is the audience?
  • What is its purpose?
  • What form does it take?

Bouchoux discusses the office memorandum at length.

Statement of Facts

  • identify parties and nature of dispute
  • chronological order
  • material facts only

Questions Presented

  • questions of law
  • material facts
  • answerable yes/no

Brief Answer

  • answers questions presented
  • material facts
  • rationale

Discussion

  • logical order
  • topic sentences for each paragraph
  • IRAC

Conclusion

  • answers questions and provides reason
  • advises on next steps or recommendations
