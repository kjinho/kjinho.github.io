6. Outlining Responses

6.1. Purpose of Outlining

  • organizing thoughts
    • arrange law into factors/elements
    • classify facts by relevance to law
  • creating a roadmap for the final written product

6.2. Outline Format

6.2.1. Harvard Outline Form

  • Top level headings use Roman numerals (I, II, III, …)
  • Second level headings use capital letters (A, B, C, …)
  • Third level headings use Arabic numberals (1, 2, 3, …)

6.2.2. Outlining Tips

  • Full sentences help you crystallize your ideas.
  • Avoid singleton headings.

6.3. Outlining as Classification

  1. Have top-level headings for each “issue”
  2. Have second-level headings for each “element” or “factor”
  3. List cases that explain each element/factor
  4. List facts that support or go against each element/factor
