1. Outlining Overview

  • [ ] Understand the practical issue (e.g., the client wants to discharge his student loans)
  • [ ] Identify the rule from the law
  • [ ] Frame the issue using the law
  • [ ] Organize subsections based on the elements/factors of the rule
  • [ ] Classify cases and facts under each element/factor
  • [ ] State your conclusion based on your analysis

2.2. Ethical Considerations

  • Model Rule of Pro. Conduct r. 2.1 (A.B.A. 1983): “In representing a client, a lawyer shall exercise independent professional judgment and render candid advice.”
  • Model Rule of Pro. Conduct r. 5.2 (A.B.A. 1983): Responsibilities of a subordinate lawyer
    • Ethics rules bind subordinate lawyers even if they act at the direction of another
    • There is no violation if a “lawyer acts in accordance with a supervisory lawyer’s reasonable resolution of an arguable question of professional duty”

TREAT IRAC

3.4. Distinguishing “Rule” from “Explanation”

RULE
black-letter law, definitions, policy
EXPLANATION
examples from case law

3.5. Distinguishing “Explanation” from “Application”

EXPLANATION
focuses solely on case law
APPLICATION
applies the case law to your case

3.6. Explaining Cases

  • Explaining cases requires at least
    • stating the outcome and
    • stating sufficient facts for later comparison.

3.7. Applying the Law

  • Mirror the explanation with the application.
  • Use comparison phrases (e.g., similarly, by contrast, however) to highlight similarities and differences with the examples from the explanation.
