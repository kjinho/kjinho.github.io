The Romanova/Gordon matter

  • “Our firm represents Natasha Romanova. Romanova was injured while visiting an estate owned by Steven Gordon in Bristol, Connecticut.”
  • “Romanova wants to know if it even makes sense to sue Gordon for her injuries or whether Gordon has landowner immunity.”
  • “Another associate has researched the possibility of an exception to immunity for a failure to warn of potential hazards on land, and the exception clearly does not apply. ... There can be be no failure to warn, malicious or otherwise, because the signs [on the estate] are, in fact, warnings. See Burgess v. State, 74 A.3d 581, 589 (Conn. Super. Ct. 2012) (holding that there was no willful or malicious failure to warn because defendant posted warning signs of hazard).”
  • “[Y]ou should not address that exception to immunity [for failure to warn] in your research. In addition, do not consider any trespass issues. Limit your analysis to whether Gordon has landowner immunity from liability.”

How do we approach an open-ended assignment?

General Approaches

  • Understand the facts
  • Understand what the client wants to know
  • Learn the law
  • Identify the legal issue
  • Classify and organize the facts pursuant to the law
  • Identify best case law
