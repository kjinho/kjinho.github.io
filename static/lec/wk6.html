IRAC Paragraphs

The issue is whether Mr. Smith is liable after Smith’s dog Bailey bit Mrs. Green when she hit it for going to the bathroom in her yard. Courts have found that a dog owner is liable for injuries sustained if its dog bites without provocation. Barnett v. Rosen, 22 U.S. 896 (1987). In Barnett, the Court found that a neighbor provoked a dog when he hit the dog to silence its barking. Id. In our case, Mrs. Green struck Bailey with the intent to stop him from voiding in her yard. As in Barnett, the Court will likely find that Mr. Smith is not liable for Mrs. Green’s injuries because Mrs. Green intended to hit the dog and thereby provoked it.
