The Parker/Hyperion matter

  • “Our firm represents Peter Parker, a Detroit, Michigan resident. Parker currently works for Hyperion Auto Parts, Inc., an auto parts distributor headquartered in Detroit, Michigan.”
  • “Parker met with the president of Avengers, Kamala Khan, who offered Parker a position with Avengers, serving as a sales rep for metropolitan Detroit.”
  • “Parker ... is concerned that the noncompetition agreement he signed when he began working at Hyperion will prevent him from accepting the position at Avengers.”
  • “I would like you to consider whether under Michigan law, Hyperion can enforce the noncompetition agreement to prevent Parker from accepting the position with Avengers.”

How do we approach an open-ended assignment?

General Approaches

  • Understand the facts
  • Understand what the client wants to know
  • Learn the law
  • Identify the legal issue
  • Classify and organize the facts pursuant to the law
  • Identify best case law

How do we classify and organize the facts?

  • Start from the law governing NDAs between employer and employee
  • Mich. Comp. Laws § 445.774a (1987)
  • St. Clair Med., P.C. v. Borgiel, 715 N.W.2d 914 (Mich. Ct. App. 2006)
  • Elements
    • “a restrictive covenant must protect an employer’s reasonable competitive business interests”
    • “its protection in terms of duration, geographical scope, and the type of employment or line of business must be reasonable”
    • “a restrictive covenant must be reasonable as between the parties”
    • “it must not be specially injurious to the public”
