Attorney Jin-Ho King has spent his legal career solving problems. His method? Precision. When a client approaches him with a problem to solve, Mr. King looks for the facts and law that will make a difference. He zeroes in on these factors to tailor his negotiations, arguments, and court filings to highlight the winning rationale.

Mr. King is a partner at the law firm Milligan Rona Duran & King LLC. Mr. King focuses his practice in appeals to the Supreme Judicial Court, the Massachusetts Appeals Court, and the Court of Appeals for the First Circuit. He also practices in the trial courts in Massachusetts.

He represents clients at all stages of litigation, including trial and appeal. His clients have included attorneys, corporations, directors and shareholders, and private individuals.

Mr. King earned his Bachelor of Arts in English at Cornell University and his Juris Doctor with Honors at the George Washington University Law School. While in law school, he was elected and served as the editor-in-chief of the George Washington International Law Review.

Mr. King is licensed to practice in the state courts of Massachusetts and New York, the United States District Court for the District of Massachusetts, and the United States Court of Appeals for the First Circuit.

Mr. King is an active member of the American Bar Association, where he currently serves as a chair of the Criminal Justice Committee of the Young Lawyers Division and its liaison to the Death Penalty Representation Project Special Committee. Mr. King has also served as a senior content editor for the Young Lawyers Division.

Mr. King speaks both English and Chinese.
